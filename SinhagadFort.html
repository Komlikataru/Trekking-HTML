Sinhagad Fort Trek

Sinhagad Fort Trek: An Introduction

Sinhagad Fort, one of the most iconic forts in Maharashtra, is a popular trekking destination near Pune. Located in the Sahyadri mountain range at an altitude of 1,312 meters, the fort offers stunning panoramic views of the surrounding valleys and the Khadakwasla Dam. The trek is famous for its historical significance, rugged terrain, and breathtaking landscapes. The main trekking route starts from the base village, Sinhagad Paytha, and takes approximately 1.5 to 2 hours to reach the top. The trail consists of steep rocky paths, lush green surroundings, and ancient stone steps. An alternative route is also available for those who prefer a drive up to the fort entrance.

History of Sinhagad Fort

Sinhagad Fort, originally known as "Kondhana Fort," has a rich history dating back over 2,000 years. The fort has witnessed several battles, the most famous being the Battle of Sinhagad in 1670, where Tanaji Malusare, a trusted commander of Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj, recaptured the fort from the Mughal army. The phrase "Gad aala, pan Sinha gela" ("The fort is won, but the lion is lost") originated from this battle, referring to Tanaji’s heroic sacrifice. Over the years, the fort was ruled by different dynasties, including the Marathas, Mughals, and British. Today, it stands as a symbol of Maharashtra’s valor and heritage.
